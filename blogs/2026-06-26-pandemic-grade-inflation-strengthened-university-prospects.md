Using teacher-assigned A-Level grades during the COVID-19 pandemic disproportionately improved the university prospects of private school students but also had a surprise “levelling” effect on disadvantaged applicants, a study published in Oxford Economic Papers has found. The University of Cambridge research is the first to provide empirical evidence of how the chaotic 2020 switch to teacher-assigned grading affected students’ trajectories into higher education.
